Our Services

LTS Social Care Services endeavours to provide the highest quality outcome focused, flexible and tailor made services. Your services will be designed to meet and respond to your individual and changing needs, support and promote your rights, choices, independence and quality of life. We provide our services from 2 hours a day through to 24 hours, 7 days a week. We provide flexible approach to caring. With our services, you can enjoy the independence of living in your own home with reassurance that your needs will be met.

Personal Care

We will support you with all activities of daily living such as washing/bathing, dressing/undressing, Oral care, Continence care management, Meal preparation / Diet monitoring, Eating and Drinking, Administering or prompting medication. To maximise service users time and reduce the impact of travel time, we schedule our staff to work within small geographic areas

Companion Care

LTS believes that with adequate support everyone can lead a happy, healthy and stimulating life. You will be matched with a companion taking into consideration your requirements and personality. The aim of this services is to minimise the risk of social isolation and loneliness. Your allocated companion will support you continue to engage in social activities that are meaningful to you. This might include but not limited to going out for a meal on your birthday, general companionship and conversation, cooking and sharing your favourite meal and driving you to places you would like to go. Based on your likes, the companion will suggest activities for you to do

Supported Living

The support is offered on a 24/7 full time basis with staff either sleeping over in your home or staying awake overnight. LTS Social Care Services will support you to independently and safely remain in you own home. As part of your living arrangement, you will be supported with the basic and physical activities of daily living to live an ordinary life as possible, this could be help with:

  • Managing bills and money
  • Cooking and healthy eating
  • Getting a job
  • Shopping
  • Learning new skills for independence
  • Personal care and well-being
  • Managing medication
  • Accessing employment, sports and socia activities
  • Attending college/university
  • Attending medical/hospital appointments
